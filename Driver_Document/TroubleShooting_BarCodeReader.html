Troubleshooting Guide for Barcode Reader - Cognex DM282X

This guide helps diagnose and resolve common hardware, desktop application, and driver issues with the Barcode Reader - Cognex DM282X

Hardware-Trouble Shooting Guide for Barcode Reader - Cognex DM282X

Understanding LED Indicators and Troubleshooting Power Issues:

Issue Possible Cause Solution
LED turns RED, blinking The scanner consistently fails to read barcodes 1.Check Barcode Print Quality: Ensure the barcode is not faded, smudged, or damaged.
2.Increase Scan Time or Retry Attempts: Adjust timeout settings in the DataMan Setup Tool.
3.Adjust Illumination Settings: Poor lighting conditions may impact readability.
4.Check Contrast Between Barcode and Background: Ensure a clear contrast for better recognition.
LED turns to green or red LED but not blinking after scanning. User Indicator LEDs Not Functioning as Expected. 1.Check Scanner Alignment: Ensure the barcode is positioned correctly.
2.Barcode Quality: Dirty, damaged, or poorly printed barcodes may not scan properly.
3.Check Scanner Lens: Clean the scanner lens to remove dust or smudges.
4.Increase Exposure Time: If lighting conditions are poor, adjust the scanner’s exposure settings.
Read LED turns to green but not blinking The scanner does not indicate a successful scan. 1.Ensure Barcode is Within Range: Move the barcode closer or further away from the scanner.
2.Check Barcode Symbology Settings: Confirm the barcode format is supported and enabled in the DataMan Setup Tool.
3.Verify Focus and Angle: Adjust the scanner’s angle to avoid glare or reflections.
4.Check for Firmware Updates: An outdated firmware version may cause reading issues—update using the DataMan Setup Tool.
Inputs (Green, Solid) 24V DC power is supplied with no faults Set the camera to a Static IP using Pylon IP Configurator.
White LED Not Turning On for Identify Function The Identify function does not activate. 1.Confirm DataMan Setup Tool is Configured
2.Correctly: Open the Identify function settings and verify the mode.
3.Check LED Settings: Some configurations may disable certain indicator LEDs—enable them in the Setup Tool.
Power Indicator LED is OFF The barcode reader does not turn on. 1.Check Power Connection: Ensure the device is plugged into a working power source.
2.Test with Another Power Adapter: A faulty power adapter may be preventing the device from turning on.
3.Check for Hardware Issues: If power does not restore, the device may have internal damage.
Train/Trigger Status LED Not Turning On The barcode reader is not recognizing a trained code. Ensure a Code is Trained: Train the scanner with a known barcode via the DataMan Setup Tool. Reset Training and Reconfigure: If a previously trained code is not detected, retrain the scanner.
Communication LED is OFF (No Ethernet Connection) The device is not connecting to the network. 1.Check Network Cables: Ensure the Ethernet cable is securely plugged in.
2.Test Network Connectivity: Try pinging the scanner’s IP address to verify communication.
3.Check IP Configuration: Ensure the device has the correct static IP or DHCP settings.
4.Verify Firewall and Network Restrictions: Some networks block unauthorized devices
Error LED is ON (Device Detected an Error) The barcode reader indicates an internal issue. 1.Restart the Device: Power cycle the barcode reader and check if the issue persists.
2.Check Error Codes in DataMan Setup Tool: Review detailed error messages to identify specific faults.
3.Update Firmware: Outdated firmware may cause errors—update through Cognex DataMan Setup Tool.
4.Factory Reset: If issues persist, reset the device to factory settings and reconfigure.
Error LED is Solid Red The barcode reader is in underpowered state 1.Verify that the 24 V DC power supply is unplugged and not receiving power
2.Attach the +24 V DC connector of the Power and I/O Breakout cable and Ground wires to the corresponding terminals on the power supply.
3.Attach the M12 connector of the Power and I/O Breakout Cable to the 24 V DC connector of the reader.
4.Restore power to the 24 V DC power supply and turn it on if necessary.

Troubleshooting Hardware and Connection Issues:

Issue Possible Cause Solution
The barcode scanner is not turning on. 1.Faulty power supply or PoE issue
2.Loose or damaged cables
3.No LED indicators		 1.Check power source and PoE injector/switch
2.Inspect power cables for damage.
3. Disconnect and reconnect power (power cycle).
4.Try Connection to different Port
Scanner Cannot Read Barcodes 1.Barcode is damaged or unclear
2.Incorrect focus or alignment
3.Poor lighting conditions
4.Incorrect symbology settings		 1.Use a clear, undamaged barcode.
2.Adjust scanner distance and angle.
3.Improve internal or external lighting
4.Enable the required barcode symbologies in settings
Slow or Delayed Scanning 1.Low exposure settings.
2.Inefficient decoding settings
3.Poor lighting
4. Network latency (for Ethernet scanners)		 1.Increase exposure time in settings.
2.Optimize decoding parameters.
3.Ensure adequate lighting.
3.Use wired Ethernet for faster response
Scanner Frequently Drops Connection 1.Loose or damaged cables .
2.Incorrect network configuration.
3.Electromagnetic interference (EMI).
4.Outdated firmware		 1.Inspect and replace faulty cables .
2.Set a static IP to avoid network conflicts.
3.Keep scanner away from high-power devices.
3.Update firmware to the latest version
Scanner Cannot Read Barcodes Barcodes printed on reflective material, causing glare. 1.Adjust the scanner's angle to minimize reflection.
2.Reduce the scanner's illumination intensity through the Cognex software, which enhances contrast without glare.

2.Reboot the scanner after updating to ensure new software takes effect.

3.Poor lighting
4. Network latency (for Ethernet scanners)		 1.Increase exposure time in settings.

3.Use wired Ethernet for faster response
