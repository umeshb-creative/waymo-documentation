Troubleshooting Guide for Triton Camera:TRI0245S-MC

This guide helps diagnose and resolve common hardware, desktop application, and driver issues with the Triton Cameras(TRI0245S-MC)

Follow the steps below to check for power, connection, overheating, and network-related problems.

Hardware-Trouble Shooting Guide

Understanding LED Indicators and Troubleshooting Power Issues:

Issue Possible Cause Solution
Flashing red
The camera is powered, but no Ethernet link is established. 		1.Loose or faulty Ethernet cable .
2.Network adapter issue .
3.Incorrect IP settings. 		1.Check and reseat the Ethernet cable.
2.Try a different Ethernet port or cable.
3.Verify that the network adapter is enabled and properly configured.
4.Ensure the PC and camera are on the same subnet.
Flashing Green
 		1.Incorrect IP configuration
2.Firewall or antivirus blocking communication
3.Camera not recognized by software 		1.Check the IP settings in the network adapter.
2.Temporarily disable firewall/antivirus and check connection.
3.Refresh the camera list in Arena View or relevant software.
Solid green
The camera is powered, the Ethernet link is established, and there is network traffic. 		Normal operation No action is needed. If the camera is still not functioning as expected, check the software settings.
Flashing red/green
Firmware update in progress. 		Firmware is being updated 1.Do not power off or disconnect the camera during the update.
2.Wait for the process to complete.
3.If it persists for an unusually long time, check firmware update logs.
Solid red
Error. The firmware update failed.		 1.Corrupted firmware
2.Interrupted firmware update
3.Hardware issue 		1.Power cycle the camera and try updating again.
2.Access the firmware recovery page: http://{your-camera-ip}/firmware-update.html.

Note 1:
The following LED sequence occurs when the camera is powered up and connected to a network:

  • LED off, plug in the Ethernet cable
  • LED on, flashing red
  • After the link is established, the LED becomes flashing green.
  • Launch the application and start capturing images, the LED becomes solid green.

Note 2:
Powering the Camera and Image Quality: :

  • The camera may not work, may be damaged, or may exhibit unintended behaviour if powered outside of the specified power range.
  • When using Power over Ethernet, the power supply must comply with the IEEE 802.3af standard.
  • When using the GPIO, the supplied power must be within the stated voltage range.
  • Dust or fingerprints on the sensor may result in a loss of image quality. Attach the dust cap to the camera when a lens is not mounted.
  • The camera may not work, may be damaged, or may exhibit unintended behaviour if operated outside of the specified temperature range (Storage Temperature -30 to 60°C and Operating Temperature -20 to 55°C ambient).

Troubleshooting Connection and Network Issues :

Issue Possible Cause Solution
Not Recognized in Windows Device Manager. Driver not installed. Install or update the driver from the official lucid website
Network Connection Lost No GigE connection. Connect the camera directly to the PC to rule out switch issues. Check for firewall restrictions.
Camera Not Accessible via IP IP conflict or wrong subnet. Use ipconfig or ping to verify the assigned IP address. Set the Jumbo Frame size to a maximum (1.5kB) for better communication.
Camera Not Detected in Arena View Application. 1.IP address allocation issue.
2.Loose or damaged Ethernet cable. 		1.Set the camera to a Static IP.
2.Check and replace the Ethernet cable. Ensure the cable supports GigE Vision.

Troubleshooting Physical Damage & Overheating Issues:

Issue Possible Cause Solution
Camera Overheating. Poor ventilation or high ambient temperature. Ensure proper cooling. Use the camera in a well-ventilated area.
Blurry Images / Focus Issues. Dust or debris on the lens. Clean the lens carefully with a microfiber cloth. Remove the lens cap before use.
Burnt Smell or Physical Damage. A power surge or short circuit. Inspect the camera for visible damage. If burnt marks are found, do not power it on. Contact support.

Reset & Recovery:

Step Action
Power Cycle Turn off the camera, wait for 10 seconds, and turn it back on.
Factory Reset Use the Arena View Software Suite to reset the camera to factory settings.
